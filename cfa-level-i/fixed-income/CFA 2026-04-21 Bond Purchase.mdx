Q. When purchase a bond with face value $1,000, how much do I pay now? Assuming the bond matured in 1 year, and coupon rate is the interest rate that bond pays. 

## Suppose the interest rate is 3% and the coupon rate is 0 (aka zero-coupon bond)

- What I will receive is 
the face value $1,000.

- What I pay now is 
$1000/(1+0.03)^1$ = 970.9 

and the profit is 1000-970.9 = 29.1 

## Suppose the interest rate is 3% and the coupon rate is 2%

- What I will receive is 
1,000 + 1000 x 2% = 1,020

- What I pay now is 
$1020/(1+0.03)^1$ = 990.3  

Terminology:

- **trading at par**: coupon rate = the market yield (note that this is equally to say, what I pay now for the bond = the face value of the bond) 
- **trading at a premium**: coupon rate > the market yield
- **trading at a discount**: coupon rate < the market yield
