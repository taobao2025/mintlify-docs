## Capital Asset Pricing Model

The model measures the relationship between expected return and systematic risk:

$E(R_i) = R_f + \beta \left(E(R_m) - R_f\right)$

where $\beta$ measures the systematic risk relative to the market, and the model assumes that investors hold diversified portfolios. 

systematic risk, aka market risk, such as interest rate changes, geopolitical events, cannot be diversified away.
un-systematic risk, aka specific risk, such as company management issues, product failures
